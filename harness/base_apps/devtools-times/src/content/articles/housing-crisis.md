The dream of homeownership is becoming increasingly distant for millions as a severe housing shortage grips major metropolitan areas. A combination of supply chain issues, labor shortages, and restrictive zoning laws has crippled the construction industry's ability to respond.

"We are facing a generational crisis," said one urban planning expert. "Without significant intervention to boost housing supply, we risk creating permanently divided cities where only the wealthy can afford a place to live."

Governments are under pressure to act, with proposals ranging from subsidizing new construction to overhauling zoning regulations. The challenge is immense, and the social and economic consequences of inaction are dire.
