The age of the automobile's dominance in urban centers may be coming to an end. Cities around the globe are investing billions in next-generation public transit, from high-speed rail to autonomous electric bus fleets.

The goal is twofold: to drastically reduce the carbon footprint of urban transportation and to alleviate the crippling traffic congestion that plagues daily life for millions of commuters.

These projects are not without their challenges, facing massive construction hurdles and debates over funding. However, proponents argue that they are a necessary investment in a more sustainable and livable urban future.
