The familiar sight of a daily newspaper may soon be a relic of the past. The relentless shift to digital news consumption has decimated the print media industry, leading to widespread closures of local and national publications.

While some legacy media outlets have successfully transitioned to online subscription models, many have struggled to compete with the immediacy and low cost of digital-native news sources.

This transition has profound implications for journalism, raising concerns about the future of in-depth investigative reporting and the creation of "news deserts" in communities that have lost their local papers.
