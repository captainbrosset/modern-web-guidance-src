A recent report has painted a grim picture of the state of the nation's infrastructure, grading it a D+ and highlighting thousands of structurally deficient bridges and roads in critical need of repair.

The consequences of this decay are not just economic, leading to costly traffic congestion and supply chain disruptions. They are also a matter of public safety, as the risk of bridge collapses, dam failures, and water main breaks grows with each passing year.

A massive, multi-trillion dollar investment is needed to modernize these vital systems, but political gridlock has stalled progress. "We are living on borrowed time," the report's author warned. "We cannot afford to wait for a disaster to force our hand."
