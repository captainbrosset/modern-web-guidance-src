The labor movement is experiencing a resurgence of energy and militancy not seen in decades. Emboldened by a tight labor market and fueled by frustration over stagnant wages, workers in various sectors are walking off the job and onto the picket lines.

These strikes are not just about pay; they are about dignity, respect, and a sense of fairness. Many workers feel that they have been left behind as corporations have reaped record profits.

The outcome of these labor disputes could have a lasting impact on the balance of power between capital and labor, potentially ushering in a new era of worker empowerment.
